Usage

  • On your 3DS -> InputProc -> Boot NTR Selector -> Wait until its back to the Home menu.
  • On kit-kat -> Enter 3DS IP Address
  • Open Controller Tab -> Wait for InputRedirection Client to Load -> Press ESC -> Press f4 -> OK
  • On Connect Tab -> Hit Connect -> Give it a few seconds to Open NtrViewer.exe
  • Hover the Touchscreen over Ntrviewer's Touchscreen
  • Load Vitrite.exe -> Press Cntrl+Shift+1 to Turn kit-kat-touch.exe Transparant

Clicks now Register on NtrViewer.exe as Physical Touchscreen Input!

Use Joy2Key to Map a Gamepad's Right Control Stick to your Mouse Controls = Full Gamepad Control over your 3DS (minus c-Stick)


Setting Up Joy2Key for Gamepad Mouse Control
Joy2key can map near any gamepad to keyboard/mouse Inputs
(useful when games only have keyboard controls, you can map buttons to those keys and have a working gamepad)
If you have your 3DS near your Modem and play with a Controller, then you might need to;
make choices on the touchscreen of the 3DS.

- If you are lazy like me, instead of leaning over to use the Touchscreen on the 3DS 
- You'd route the Second Joystick to the Mouse + Left Click on your PC


Input Redirection & Joy2Key both Natively Supports XInput Controllers (Xbox One + 360).
This means we can easily use any controller thanks to:
ds4windows.com - Turn Dualshock Controllers into XInput
wiinupro.com - WiiU + Wii Controllers to XInput (Tutorial here)

  • Download J2K from within kit-kat-touch's Download Tab


  • Extract & Open Joy2Key.exe

  • Click the Options Tab 
  • Click Configure 
  • Click "2. Advanced settings for each device" - to find your XInput Device
 (if using WiinUSoft or ds4windows make sure you pick XInput Device not your Dualschock or Wiiupro Gamepad)


To turn a Joystick into Mouse Inputs we need to change Stick2 to Mouse Directions
  • Select Stick2 and the Direction you need
  • Click Edit Button Assignment


  • Click the Mouse Tab 
  • Slide the Curser movement needles in the Corresponding Direction of Stick2


  • Repeat for All 4 Directions


Now we need to Select a Button to Register as Mouse Clicks/Stylus Taps

 Pressing the Button you Want to Use as Stylus Taps 
- will register as a a Highlighted Yellow Button

  • Click Said Button Number (10 in my Case)
  • Click "Edit Button Assignment"
  • Select Left Mouse Click under the Mouse Tab
  • Click Ok and Minimize Joy2Key to the taskbar 
Your Right Gamepad Control Stick Should now Control your Mouse - Allowing you to:
- Click kit-kat-touch's InputRedirectionClient Window (Hovering over NTRViewer's Touchscreen) without getting up!
